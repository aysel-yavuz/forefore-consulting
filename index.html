CASE STUDIES

A global financial company needed to find a browser-based PDF viewer with smart redacting capabilities. They were about o purchase one based on nothing more than influence from advertising. However, one of our advisors performed an thorough research, lisiting all products in the market and taking notes of all of their features, their prices, and giving score for the quality of other aspects such as documention, customer support, community size, compatibility with the company's techstack, and dozens of other criteria. This report was used to evaluate the potential of each solution to satisfy not only the current project, but also future projects. Finally, the report also described what it would take to create an in-house solution and the steps to make that happen. The leadersgip of the company loved the report and was able to make an educated choice, choosing the most optimal solution, balancing scope, quality, price, and development speed, which saved thousands of dollars for that project and many consecutive projects since then.
A global fin-tech company needed to create a robust, lean and performant UI Component Library but wasn't sure on how to make it that way while still supporting multiple lines of business (LOB). Each LOB could potentally require different (a) features, (b) release and maintenance schedule, (c) theme, (d) types of web developers, (e) timezones and languages to support, etc. How could we make it so versitile and yet keep it lean and easy to use? One of Forefront's advisors, successfully designed the solution, created the dev-enviroment, coded the first few components as examples, trained the team, stablished a custom workflow, and invented an self-documentation solution that automatically creates a portal website, favoring discoverability. The initiative was so successful that it grew into a Frontend Shop. It also revolutionized the company's Design System, making it possible to stablish a strong visual identity across all lines of businesses and reducing development time in around 30%. The UI Library also enabled that high-fideliy prototypes could be created in just few days, briging a tremendous business advantage for the organization.
A global restaurant chain was looking for an innovative method of cleaning which would guarantee their insurance provider that they were eliminating their number one cause of claims: slip-n-fall.
One of our advisors created three models of "smart mops" and "smart brooms" which would connect to the local manager's computer, letting them know the frequency and the areas the areas covered with cleaning during the work hours. The idea was the the grounds should be cleaned even if no visible spills were found, because they could go unnoticed and lead to accidents. With the use of those smart devices, besides saving money with their insurance premiums, the restaurant chain also reduced the number of accidents.
